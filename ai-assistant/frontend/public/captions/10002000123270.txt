Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement. It is the second most common neurodegenerative disorder after Alzheimer's disease.

The symptoms of Parkinson's disease include:

Tremor
Rigidity
Bradykinesia
Postural instability
The disease is caused by a loss of dopamine-producing cells in the brain. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter that helps control movement.

The risk factors for Parkinson's disease include:

Age
Family history of the disease
Exposure to certain toxins
Head injury
The diagnosis of Parkinson's disease is based on the patient's symptoms and a physical examination. There is no single test that can definitively diagnose the disease.

The treatment for Parkinson's disease includes:

Medication
Surgery
Physical therapy
Speech therapy
The goal of treatment is to relieve the symptoms of the disease and improve the patient's quality of life.

The prognosis for Parkinson's disease is variable. The disease can progress slowly or rapidly. Most people with Parkinson's disease can live a full and active life.
