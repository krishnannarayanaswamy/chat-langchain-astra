 **17 ULCERATIVE COLITIS**
Ulcerative colitis is characterized by inflammation and ulcers in the digestive tract.
The disease generally has a chronic progression.
Ulcerative colitis is found usually only in the innermost lining of the large intestine (colon) and rectum.

**Epidemiology**
- Ulcerative colitis is classified as Common.
- It affects more than 1 million cases per year in India.

**Etiology**
The exact cause of ulcerative colitis is yet unknown, but there are certain factors that appear to trigger or aggravate it.
- Diet
- Drugs
- Infections
- Stress
- Genetic Tendency
- Allergens

**Diagnosis**
- The initial diagnostic workup for ulcerative colitis consists multiple factors: a complete history of the patient, a thorough physical examination, assessing signs and symptoms, lab tests and endoscopy.
- Biopsy sample (H&E stain) that demonstrates marked lymphocytic infiltration (blue/purple) of the intestinal mucosa and architectural distortion of the crypts:

**Signs and Symptoms**
- Bloody Stool
- Diarrhea
- Rectal Pain
- Abdominal Pain
- Constant Urgency to Defecate
- Weight Loss
- Chronic Fatigue
- Nutritional deficiencies
- Frequent fevers
- Dehydration
- Canker Sores
- Eye Pain
- Joint Pain
- Anemia
- Liver diseases
- Mucous or Pus in stool

**Treatment**
- Standard treatment for ulcerative colitis depends on the extent of involvement and severity of the disease.
- The goal is to induce remission initially with medications, followed by the administration of maintenance medications to prevent a relapse.
