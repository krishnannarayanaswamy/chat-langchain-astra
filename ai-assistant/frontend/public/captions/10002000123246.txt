## Treatment
- Educate patients about their symptoms & physiology of chronic pain and the importance of non-pharmacologic therapies.
- Land or water-based exercise improves function, pain, stiffness, muscle strength, and fitness
- Tai chi, yoga, qigong, and a variety of other therapies have been reported to be safe and effective for overall symptoms and physical functioning.
- Cognitive therapy helps persons self-manage and reframe worrisome and intrusive thoughts
- Fibromyalgia treatments include both medication and self-care measures. The focus is on reducing symptoms and enhancing overall health. While there isn't a single cure for all symptoms, using a number of approaches can help in the long run.
- Medications can help reduce the pain of Fibromyalgia and improve sleep.
- Muscle relaxants, Cyclobenzaprine, Antidepressants (balanced serotonin-nor-epinephrine reuptake inhibitors), Amitriptyline, Duloxetine, Pregabalin & Milnacipran (FDA approved) are Pharmacologic Agents Effective for the treatment of Fibromyalgia.
