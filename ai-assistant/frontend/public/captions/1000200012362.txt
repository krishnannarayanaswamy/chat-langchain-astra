 # **18. CHROHN'S DISEASE**
+ Crohn's disease is a chronic inflammatory bowel disease.
+ It affects the digestive tract lining.
+ It causes tissue inflammation in the digestive tract, which leads to abdominal pain, severe diarrhea, fatigue, weight loss and malnutrition.
+ Although treatments can help, Crohn's disease can't be cured permanently.
+ The disease was named after gastroenterologist Burrill Bernard Crohn, who first described the disease in 1932.

## **Epidemiology**
+ Crohn's disease is classified as Rare.
+ It affects fewer than 1 million cases per year in India.

## **Etiology**
+ The exact cause of ulcerative colitis is unknown, but there are specific factors that appear to trigger or aggravate it.

**Immune System**
+ Disrupted microflora-immune response
+ Th1> Th 2

**Genetics**
+ Polygenic disease
+ Susceptible loci
+ Positive Fhx
+ HLA gene associations

**Environment**
+ Alteration in bacterial microflora
+ Nutritional & social factors

## **Diagnosis**
+ The diagnosis of Crohn's disease is challenging at times, and many tests are often required to assist the physician in making the diagnosis.
+ A colonoscopy has a 70% chance to correctly diagnose the disease.
