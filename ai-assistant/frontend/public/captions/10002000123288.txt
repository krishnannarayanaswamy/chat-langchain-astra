Epidemiology of Malaria
Malaria is more common in warm, humid climates with frequent rainfall. It is transmitted through the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito.

Agent of Malaria
The agent of Malaria is a protozoan parasite called Plasmodium. There are four different species of Plasmodium that can cause Malaria in humans: P. falciparum, P. vivax, P. ovale, and P. malariae.

Symptoms of Malaria
The symptoms of Malaria can vary depending on the species of Plasmodium that causes the infection. However, the most common symptoms include fever, chills, sweating, headache, muscle aches, and fatigue. In severe cases, Malaria can lead to anemia, kidney failure, and death.

Incubation Period of Malaria
The incubation period of Malaria is the time between the bite of an infected mosquito and the appearance of symptoms. The incubation period varies depending on the species of Plasmodium that causes the infection. For P. falciparum, the incubation period is typically 9-14 days. For P. vivax, the incubation period is typically 8-17 days. For P. ovale, the incubation period is typically 16-18 days. And for P. malariae, the incubation period is typically 18-40 days.
