 Lichen Planus is a chronic inflammatory skin condition that affects the skin, nails, and mucous membranes. It is characterized by the presence of small, itchy, purple bumps that often appear in clusters. The most common sites affected are the wrists, ankles, and lower back.

The exact cause of Lichen Planus is unknown, but it is thought to be related to an autoimmune reaction. This means that the body's immune system mistakenly attacks its own tissues.

Lichen Planus can be treated with a variety of medications, including topical steroids, oral steroids, and light therapy. In some cases, the condition may go away on its own without treatment.

Symptoms of Lichen Planus

Dry, scaly skin

Treatment for Lichen Planus

There is no cure for Lichen Planus, but there are a variety of treatments that can help to relieve symptoms. The most common treatments include:

Topical steroids: These are creams or ointments that are applied directly to the skin. They can help to reduce inflammation and itching.
Oral steroids: These are pills that are taken by mouth. They are used to treat more severe cases of Lichen Planus.
Light therapy: This treatment involves exposing the skin to ultraviolet light. It can help to reduce inflammation and itching.
In some cases, Lichen Planus may go away on its own without treatment. However, it is important to see a doctor if you have symptoms of Lichen Planus so that you can get the appropriate treatment.

Prognosis of Lichen Planus

The prognosis of Lichen Planus is generally good. Most people with the condition can expect to experience relief from symptoms with treatment. However, some people may experience flare-ups of the condition from time to time.
