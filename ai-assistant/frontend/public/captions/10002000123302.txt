 The image is about ADHD. It starts with the definition of ADHD, which is a mental health condition that can cause unusual levels of hyperactivity and impulsive behaviors. It is more commonly seen in children, but can also be seen in adults. 

There are three different types of ADHD:
- Combined ADHD
- Inattentive ADHD
- Hyperactive-Impulsive ADHD

The etiology of ADHD is not fully understood, but it is thought to be caused by a combination of genetic and environmental factors. 

Risk factors for ADHD include:
- Inherited genetic vulnerability
- Biologically mediated risk factors such as low birth weight and parental exposure to toxins
- Childhood exposure to chemicals such as lead and pesticides
- Physiological risk factors such as difficult parent-child interactions, unequipped school settings, and unhealthy peer relationships

The diagnosis of ADHD is based on a specific assessment that looks for symptoms related to aggression, hyperactivity, conduct issues, attention problems, learning issues, anxiety, and depression. 

The signs and symptoms of ADHD in children and teenagers include:
- Difficulty sustaining attention while completing long tasks
- Failing to pay close attention to details
- Difficulty organizing tasks and activities
- Forgetfulness
- Appearing to be unable to listen when spoken to directly
- Not able to follow instructions properly
- Getting distracted easily
- Constant fidgeting
- Talking excessively
- Impulsive nature

The treatment for ADHD typically involves a combination of medication and behavioral therapy. 

Medication can help to improve attention and focus, while behavioral therapy can help to teach children and teenagers how to manage their symptoms.
