 The image is a diagram of the general anatomy of the nervous system. It includes the central nervous system (CNS) and the peripheral nervous system (PNS). The CNS is composed of the brain and the spinal cord, while the PNS is composed of the nerves that connect the CNS to the rest of the body.

The brain is the center of the nervous system and is responsible for controlling all of the body's functions. It is divided into two hemispheres, the left and right hemispheres, which are connected by the corpus callosum. The left hemisphere is responsible for controlling the right side of the body, while the right hemisphere is responsible for controlling the left side of the body.

The spinal cord is a long, thin tube of nervous tissue that runs from the brain down the back. It is responsible for carrying messages between the brain and the rest of the body.

The PNS is composed of the nerves that connect the CNS to the rest of the body. These nerves are responsible for carrying messages from the brain to the muscles and organs, and for carrying sensory information from the body to the brain.
