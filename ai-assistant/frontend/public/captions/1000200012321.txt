**Definition**
Leukemia is a type of cancer that starts in the blood-forming tissue of the bone marrow. It is characterized by the rapid production of abnormal white blood cells, which can interfere with the body's ability to fight infection.

**Types of Leukemia**
There are four main types of leukemia:
- Acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL)
- Chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL)
- Acute myeloid leukemia (AML)
- Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML)

**ALL** is the most common type of leukemia in children. It is characterized by the rapid production of immature lymphocytes, which are a type of white blood cell. ALL can be treated with chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and stem cell transplant.
**CLL** is the most common type of leukemia in adults. It is characterized by the slow production of mature lymphocytes. CLL can be treated with chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and targeted therapy.
**AML** is a type of leukemia that is characterized by the rapid production of immature myeloid cells, which are a type of white blood cell. AML can be treated with chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and stem cell transplant.
**CML** is a type of leukemia that is characterized by the slow production of mature myeloid cells. CML can be treated with chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and targeted therapy.

**Causes of Leukemia**
The exact cause of leukemia is unknown, but there are a number of risk factors that can increase the risk of developing the disease. These risk factors include:
- Exposure to radiation
- Exposure to certain chemicals
- Having a family history of leukemia
- Having a weakened immune system

**Treatment Options for Leukemia**
The treatment options for leukemia depend on the type of leukemia, the stage of the disease, and the patient's age and overall health. Treatment options may include:
- Chemotherapy
- Radiation therapy
- Targeted therapy
- Stem cell transplant
