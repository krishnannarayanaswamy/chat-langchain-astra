A heart attack occurs when the blood flow to the heart is blocked, usually by a blood clot. This can cause chest pain, shortness of breath, and other symptoms. Heart attacks are more common in men than in women, but they can be just as serious for women. In fact, women are more likely to die from a heart attack than men.

There are a number of things that can increase your risk of having a heart attack, such as high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, and obesity. Women who are pregnant or have recently given birth are also at an increased risk.

If you think you are having a heart attack, it is important to call 911 immediately. Do not drive yourself to the hospital. The sooner you get to the hospital, the sooner you can receive treatment and the better your chances of survival.

Treatment for a heart attack may include medications to dissolve the blood clot, surgery to open the blocked artery, and lifestyle changes to reduce your risk of having another heart attack.
