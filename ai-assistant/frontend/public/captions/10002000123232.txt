The next step is to determine if the patient has signs of lower motor neuron (LMN) or upper motor neuron (UMN) involvement. LMN signs include muscle weakness, atrophy, and fasciculations. UMN signs include spasticity, hyperreflexia, and clonus.

If the patient has signs of both LMN and UMN involvement, then the next step is to determine the distribution of the signs. If the signs are confined to one region of the body, then the patient is diagnosed with possible ALS. If the signs are present in two or more regions of the body, then the patient is diagnosed with probable ALS.

If the patient has signs of only LMN involvement, then the next step is to determine the duration of the symptoms. If the symptoms have been present for less than two months, then the patient is diagnosed with suspected ALS. If the symptoms have been present for two months or more, then the patient is diagnosed with probable ALS.

If the patient has signs of only UMN involvement, then the patient is diagnosed with possible ALS.
