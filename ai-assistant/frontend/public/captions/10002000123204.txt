Erectile dysfunction is the inability to achieve or maintain an erection sufficient for satisfactory sexual performance.

**Symptoms**
- Difficulty in achieving an erection
- Difficulty in maintaining an erection
- Reduced sexual desire

**Causes**
There are two main types of erectile dysfunction:
- Organic erectile dysfunction is caused by a physical problem with the penis or the blood vessels that supply it.
- Psychogenic erectile dysfunction is caused by a psychological problem, such as stress, anxiety, or depression.

**Risk factors**
- Age
- Diabetes
- Heart disease
- High blood pressure
- Obesity
- Smoking
- Alcoholism
- Drug use
- Certain medications

**Treatment**
The treatment for erectile dysfunction depends on the cause.
- Organic erectile dysfunction can be treated with medications, such as sildenafil (Viagra), tadalafil (Cialis), and vardenafil (Levitra). 
- In some cases, surgery may be necessary.
- Psychogenic erectile dysfunction can be treated with therapy, such as cognitive-behavioral therapy. 

**Prevention**
There are no surefire ways to prevent erectile dysfunction, but there are some things you can do to reduce your risk, such as:
- Exercising regularly
- Eating a healthy diet
- Maintaining a healthy weight
- Quitting smoking
- Limiting alcohol intake
- Managing stress
- Avoiding certain medications that can cause erectile dysfunction
