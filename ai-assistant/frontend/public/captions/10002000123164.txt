 **Glycogen Metabolism**
Glycogen is a branched polymer of glucose that serves as a short-term energy store in the liver and skeletal muscle. It is synthesized from glucose in a process called glycogenesis and broken down into glucose in a process called glycogenolysis.

**Glycogenolysis**
Glycogenolysis is the process by which glycogen is broken down into glucose. It occurs in the liver and skeletal muscle. The steps of glycogenolysis are as follows:

1. Glycogen is phosphorylated by glycogen phosphorylase to form glucose-1-phosphate.
2. Glucose-1-phosphate is isomerized to glucose-6-phosphate.
3. Glucose-6-phosphate is dephosphorylated by glucose-6-phosphatase to form glucose.
4. Glucose is released into the bloodstream.
