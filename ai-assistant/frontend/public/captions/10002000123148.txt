**Nutrition**

A balanced diet is important for bone health. Calcium and vitamin D are two essential nutrients for bone health. Calcium is found in dairy products, dark leafy greens, and fortified foods. Vitamin D is found in fatty fish, eggs, and fortified milk.

**Lifestyle**

Smoking and excessive alcohol consumption can contribute to osteoporosis. Regular exercise can help to maintain bone mass.

**Exercise**

Weight-bearing exercises, such as walking, running, and dancing, can help to maintain bone mass. Resistance training can also help to build muscle mass, which can help to protect bones.

**Medications**

There are a number of medications that can be used to treat osteoporosis. These medications include bisphosphonates, teriparatide, and raloxifene. The type of medication that is best for a particular patient will depend on their individual needs.

**Did You Know?**

The term "osteoporosis" was coined by French pathologist Jean Lobstein in the 19th century. Hip fractures are the most serious consequence of osteoporosis.
