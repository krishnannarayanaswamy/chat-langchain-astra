 **Image 20.1**

The image shows the anatomy of the respiratory system. The respiratory system is a system of organs that work together to get oxygen into the body and remove carbon dioxide. The main organs of the respiratory system are the lungs, which are located in the chest. The lungs are made up of millions of tiny air sacs called alveoli. The alveoli are lined with capillaries, which are tiny blood vessels. Oxygen from the air passes through the alveoli and into the capillaries. The capillaries then carry the oxygen to the heart, which pumps it to the rest of the body.

The respiratory system also includes the nose, pharynx, larynx, trachea, and bronchi. The nose is the first part of the respiratory system that air passes through. The pharynx is a tube that connects the nose to the larynx. The larynx is a tube that connects the pharynx to the trachea. The trachea is a tube that connects the larynx to the lungs. The bronchi are tubes that branch off from the trachea and lead to the lungs.

The respiratory system is a vital part of the body. It provides the body with the oxygen it needs to function properly. The respiratory system also helps to remove carbon dioxide from the body. Carbon dioxide is a waste product that is produced by the cells of the body. If carbon dioxide is not removed from the body, it can build up and cause problems.

**Surfactant**

Surfactant is a substance that helps to reduce the surface tension of the alveoli. Surface tension is a force that pulls the molecules of a liquid together. In the alveoli, surface tension can cause the alveoli to collapse. Surfactant helps to prevent the alveoli from collapsing by reducing the surface tension.

Surfactant is produced by the cells of the alveoli. It is made up of a mixture of proteins and lipids. Surfactant is released into the alveoli, where it spreads out and forms a thin layer on the surface of the alveoli. This layer of surfactant helps to reduce the surface tension of the alveoli and prevents them from collapsing.

Surfactant is essential for the proper functioning of the respiratory system. Without surfactant, the alveoli would collapse and the lungs would not be able to function properly.
