Cystic fibrosis is a genetic disorder that affects the lungs, pancreas, and other organs. It is caused by a mutation in the CFTR gene, which leads to the production of a defective protein that impairs the transport of chloride ions across cell membranes. This results in the accumulation of thick, sticky mucus in the lungs, pancreas, and other organs, which can lead to a variety of health problems.

In the lungs, the mucus can block the airways and make it difficult to breathe. This can lead to recurrent chest infections, bronchiectasis (a condition in which the airways become permanently damaged), and respiratory failure. In the pancreas, the mucus can block the ducts that carry digestive enzymes to the small intestine. This can lead to malabsorption of nutrients and malnutrition. In other organs, the mucus can cause problems such as liver disease, kidney disease, and infertility.

Cystic fibrosis is a serious disease, but there are a number of treatments that can help to improve the symptoms and prevent complications. These treatments include airway clearance techniques, medications to thin the mucus, antibiotics to treat infections, and pancreatic enzyme supplements to help with digestion. With early diagnosis and treatment, most people with cystic fibrosis can live full and productive lives.
