 The image is a table that summarizes the clinical features of epiglottitis. Epiglottitis is a rapidly progressive infection of the epiglottis, which is a flap of tissue that covers the windpipe. The infection is usually caused by bacteria, such as Streptococcus pneumoniae.

Symptoms of epiglottitis include:
- Sudden onset of sore throat
- Difficulty swallowing
- Drooling
- Hoarseness of voice
- Stridor (a high-pitched, noisy breathing sound)
- Rapid progression of symptoms
- Fever
- Malaise
- Lethargy

Epiglottitis is a medical emergency and can be fatal if not treated promptly. Treatment typically involves antibiotics and hospitalization.
