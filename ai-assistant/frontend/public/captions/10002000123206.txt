BPH is a condition in which the prostate gland enlarges, causing problems with urination. It is a common condition in older men.

The prostate gland is a small gland located just below the bladder in men. It produces fluid that helps to make semen. The urethra, which is the tube that carries urine out of the body, passes through the prostate gland.

As men age, the prostate gland often enlarges. This can cause the urethra to become narrower, making it more difficult to urinate. BPH can also cause the bladder to become overactive, leading to symptoms such as frequent urination and urgency.

The symptoms of BPH can range from mild to severe. Some men may only have mild symptoms, while others may have more severe symptoms that interfere with their daily activities.

The symptoms of BPH include:

Difficulty urinating
Weak stream of urine
Hesitancy in starting to urinate
Feeling that the bladder is not completely empty after urinating
Frequent urination
Nocturia (waking up at night to urinate)
Urgency (feeling a sudden need to urinate)
Incontinence (loss of bladder control)
BPH can also lead to complications such as:

Urinary tract infections
Bladder stones
Kidney damage
In some cases, BPH can also lead to acute urinary retention, which is a condition in which the bladder is unable to empty itself completely. This can be a very painful condition and can lead to serious complications.

The treatment for BPH depends on the severity of the symptoms. In some cases, lifestyle changes such as avoiding caffeine and alcohol, and limiting fluid intake in the evening, can help to relieve symptoms. In other cases, medications may be necessary to treat BPH. Surgery may also be an option for some men with BPH.

BPH is a common condition, but it can be managed with treatment. If you are experiencing any of the symptoms of BPH, talk to your doctor.
